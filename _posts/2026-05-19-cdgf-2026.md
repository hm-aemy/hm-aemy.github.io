We presented our projects at the
[Chipdesign Germany Forum](https://www.chipdesign-germany.de/de/events/2026/dresden-chipdesign-germany-forum/)
in Dresden.

## Chipdesign Germany

This year, our group participated in the Chipdesign Germany Forum, a flagship
event that brings together chip design experts from across Germany to share the
latest advances in research. The conference was an excellent opportunity to
engage with the community and to witness the growing role that open-source tools
and methodologies play in shaping the state of the art.

The two keynotes were among the highlights of the event.
Christian Ospelkaus delivered an overview of ion-based quantum processors,
while Laurence W. Nagel presented "The Life of SPICE" — a fascinating account of
the origin and evolution of the very first EDA tool.


## Our Contributions to the Event

We presented our work within the [DE:Sign-Initiative](https://www.elektronikforschung.de/fokusthemen/designinitiative).
Prof. Dr. Stefan Wallentowitz, together with Johanna Wallenborn (GI), presented
the project **DI-OCDCpro** "From Challenge to Chip: Hands-on Learning with the
LAYR Open Chip Challenge". This was followed by a talk by Tobias Wölfel on
**DI-OSVISE** "Verification of Instruction Set Extensions made easy".
